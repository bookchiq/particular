# Research consent and data handling

## Plain-language participant summary

Particular is researching how ensemble directors select and adapt music for mixed-ability groups. Participation involves a conversation about professional experience and may include an optional review of a future prototype. Participation is voluntary. A participant may skip questions, stop at any time, or request withdrawal of their identifiable research data before it has been aggregated.

This document is a project operating template, not a substitute for institutional, school-district, or legal review. Researchers must follow any applicable organizational research, safeguarding, privacy, and records policies before recruitment.

## Consent checklist

Before an interview, obtain affirmative consent for each applicable item:

- participation in the interview;
- researcher note-taking;
- audio or video recording, requested separately and optional;
- recontact about prototype evaluation, optional;
- receipt of a score for evaluation, governed by a separate score-intake confirmation.

Record consent in the private research system with a participant code, document version, date, and permitted uses. Do not store signatures, names, email addresses, recordings, or consent records in git.

## What we collect

Collect only what is needed to answer the research questions:

- the participant's general role and ensemble context;
- descriptions of repertoire selection and part-adaptation workflows;
- approximate frequency, time, cost, and consequences of the problem;
- source formats, rights constraints, trust needs, and product-evaluation feedback;
- optional contact details for follow-up, stored separately from research notes.

Do not collect student names, student performance or disability information, protected demographic data, payment information, or credentials. Redirect participants who begin disclosing identifiable student information.

## Recordings and notes

Recording is optional and requires explicit consent. Store recordings and raw notes in an approved, access-controlled location available only to the research team. Use participant codes in notes and keep the code-to-identity key separately. Do not put recordings, transcripts, raw notes, screenshots, or links granting access to them in this repository.

Define and communicate a deletion date before collection. Delete recordings after transcription or synthesis review, and delete raw identifiable notes according to that stated retention period. Aggregated, non-identifying conclusions may be retained as project documentation.

## Score material

No score is required for an interview. Never solicit or accept a score through a public issue, pull request, repository, chat transcript, or unapproved file-sharing link.

Before accepting a score, confirm privately that:

1. the contributor owns the work, the work is public domain, or the contributor has explicit authority to arrange it and provide it for this evaluation;
2. the intended uses, access group, retention period, and deletion process are understood;
3. the file contains no personal, student, account, or hidden collaboration metadata that should be removed;
4. the contributor knows the score will not be placed in the public repository or used for model training by default; and
5. withdrawal and deletion requests have a documented contact path.

The repository may contain only redistributable evaluation fixtures with documented provenance and an explicit compatible license. Private partner scores remain in access-controlled storage and are represented in git, if needed, only by a non-identifying corpus ID and aggregate metadata.

## Use and reporting

Use collected information to evaluate the problem statement, shape product requirements, select safe evaluation fixtures, and recruit design partners. Report only aggregated themes, ranges, and de-identified quotations whose wording cannot reasonably identify a participant or organization. Separate observed evidence from product conclusions and include evidence that contradicts the current Product Contract.

Do not use research material to train external models. Do not send private notes or scores to an AI service unless the participant has specifically consented to that service, use, and data policy and the project has approved the workflow.

## Risks and benefits

Potential risks include accidental disclosure of professional opinions, repertoire plans, organizational information, or copyrighted score material. Minimize these risks through data minimization, separate storage, access controls, short retention, and aggregate reporting. There is no guaranteed direct benefit or compensation unless separately stated during recruitment.

## Withdrawal, incidents, and questions

Recruitment material must provide a monitored project contact for questions, withdrawal, deletion, or privacy incidents. Do not publish personal contact details in this repository. If data is sent through an unapproved channel or a participant discloses sensitive information, stop processing it, restrict access, document the incident privately, and follow the applicable deletion and notification process.
