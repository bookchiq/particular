# Design partner criteria

## Purpose

Design partners help Particular test whether coordinated difficulty variants are understandable, musically responsible, and useful in a real director workflow. Partnership is research participation, not an endorsement, a promise of production access, or permission to publish scores or identities.

## Qualifying partner

A design partner should:

- direct, arrange for, or make repertoire decisions for a community, school, or youth ensemble with materially different player abilities;
- have recently adapted parts or avoided repertoire for that reason;
- be able to review MusicXML and/or rendered notation and explain instrument- and ensemble-level concerns;
- commit to at least one structured prototype review and a short follow-up discussion;
- accept that generated output requires director review and must not be used in performance without their own musical and rights checks; and
- follow their organization's safeguarding, technology, privacy, and procurement rules.

Across at least three partners, prefer variation in ensemble type, instrumentation, experience level, and access to arranging resources. A partner need not provide a score.

## Score contribution criteria

If a partner voluntarily offers a representative score, accept it only through the private intake process and only when the partner confirms that it is:

- an original work they are authorized to provide and arrange;
- a public-domain work in the relevant jurisdiction with a score edition that may lawfully be used; or
- covered by explicit permission that allows the planned adaptation and evaluation.

Prefer MusicXML or MXL with enough ensemble complexity to exercise coordinated adaptation. Record instrumentation, broad difficulty characteristics, notation features, provenance, permitted uses, retention, and deletion requirements without exposing the title or owner in public metadata when that could identify the contributor.

Do not accept student compositions without appropriate ownership and organizational approvals. Do not accept files containing identifiable student data, credentials, comments, revision history, or unrelated embedded material.

## Commitment definition

Count a design-partner commitment toward the U13 gate only after all of the following are documented in the private research system:

1. a qualifying participant completed informed consent;
2. the expected review activity and approximate time were explained;
3. the participant affirmatively agreed to review a prototype;
4. communication and withdrawal paths were provided; and
5. any organizational approvals or constraints were identified.

A positive interview reaction, mailing-list signup, referral, or hypothetical willingness does not count.

## Expected review activities

Depending on prototype maturity, ask partners to:

- review preflight findings for an authorized score;
- compare original, Foundation, Core, and Challenge passages;
- flag loss of melody, bass, harmony, rhythmic drive, cues, entrances, exposed material, or ensemble balance;
- identify instrument-specific range, technique, notation, endurance, and readability problems;
- assess explanations, warnings, locks, partial failures, and export usefulness;
- estimate correction effort and whether the result improves on their current workflow; and
- state whether they would continue evaluation and under what conditions.

Do not ask partners to expose student data or rehearse unsafe or unreviewed output.

## Project responsibilities

Particular will:

- use research material only for agreed evaluation purposes;
- keep identities, contacts, raw feedback, recordings, and private scores out of git;
- restrict access to private materials and honor stated retention and deletion terms;
- label prototype output as requiring director review;
- disclose known limitations and avoid claiming universal grade levels; and
- distinguish partner observations from product-team conclusions in synthesis.

## Exit and pause conditions

Either party may pause or end participation. Stop score processing and follow the documented deletion workflow when authorization is withdrawn. Pause evaluation if a rights question, privacy incident, organizational restriction, or musical-safety concern cannot be resolved within the agreed scope.

## Portfolio tracking

Track partners by non-identifying codes in the private research system. In the public validation record, report only aggregate commitment counts and broad context diversity. Do not publish partner names, logos, testimonials, score titles, or affiliations without separate explicit permission.
